The Definitive Guide to Cutting and Bulking





You’ve been hitting the gym for a while now and are starting to see some real progress. But now you’ve hit a plateau and aren’t sure what to do next. Should you cut to reveal those hard-earned gains or bulk up and gain more muscle? It’s a debate that rages on in gyms everywhere. This guide will walk you through the pros and cons of cutting vs bulking so you can make the best choice for your goals. We’ll look at how to cut weight in a sustainable way without losing muscle and how to bulk without just getting fat. Whether you want to hit the beach with a shredded six-pack or pack on slabs of muscle, we’ve got you covered. By the end of this, you’ll have a customized plan to take your physique to the next level. The iron is calling – are you ready to cut or bulk?




What Is the Difference Between Cutting and Bulking?






Cutting and bulking refer to two different phases of working out. When you cut, you reduce your calorie intake to lose fat while maintaining muscle. Bulking means increasing your calories to gain weight, ideally muscle mass.



Cutting phase



The cutting phase is when you want to shed fat and get those abs to show through. You’ll reduce your calories, usually by 500-1000 per day. Focus on lean proteins, lots of vegetables, some fruits and whole grains. Limit processed carbs and sugar. Do high intensity interval training and weight training to maintain muscle. The goal is to lose 1-2 pounds per week.



Bulking phase



In the bulking phase, you increase your calories, especially protein and carbs, to gain weight. Aim for 300-500 more calories per day from nutrient-dense foods. Lift heavy weights and do compound exercises like squats and bench press to stimulate muscle growth. Gain ½ to 1 pound per week. Some fat gain is inevitable, so bulk in the winter when you can hide it under layers!



The key is altering between cutting and bulking. Cut for 4-6 weeks, then bulk for 6-8 weeks. This helps you gain muscle without gaining too much fat. For the best results, cut and bulk gradually. Make small changes to your diet and routine each week. Take progress pictures and measurements to stay on track.



With consistency, you can achieve an awesome physique by strategically cutting fat and bulking up muscle. The difference between the two phases is manipulating your calories and workouts to either lose fat or gain mass. Master this, and you’ll be well on your way to your fitness goals.




Should You Bulk or Cut First?



So, you’ve been hitting the gym for a while and are starting to see some results. Now its time to take it to the next level. The big question is, should you bulk up by eating more calories or cut fat by eating fewer calories?



Bulk first



If you’re skinny, bulking up is usually the way to go. By eating more calories, especially form protein and carbs, you’ll gain muscle mass. Aim for 300-500 calories above your maintenance level and lift heavy weights 3-4 times a week. You can expect to gain 1-2 pounds of muscle per week.



Some tips on bulking:

  • 1. Focus on compound lifts like squats, deadlifts, bench press and overhead press. These exercises stimulate the most muscle growth.

  • 2. Eat protein with each meal, especially after your workout. Good options include chicken, fish, lean meat, eggs, nuts and dairy

  • 3. Increase your carb intake by eating more rice, potatoes, oats and starchy veggies. But don’t overdo it or you’ll gain excess fat

  • 4. Use supplements like protein powder and creatine to boost your gains. Creatine helps increase strength and power.

  • 5. Get plenty of rest and sleep. Muscle recovery and growth happen when you’re resting, not when you’re working out.



Cut fat



if you’ve built up some muscle but also gained extra fluff around your midsection, its time to cut. Reduce your calorie intake by 300-500 calories below maintenance. Focus on lean proteins, lots of vegetables, moderate portions and intense cardio 3 times a week. Aim to lose 1-2 pounds per week.



Some tips on cutting:

  • 1. Do high intensity interval training or cardio intervals to maximize fat burning. Try altering between high intensity bursts and recovery periods.

  • 2. Fill up on non-starchy veggies, leafy greens and lean proteins. Limit high carb foods, sweets and full-fat dairy

  • 3. Stay hydrated and drink plenty of water. This will keep you feeling full and reduce appetite.

  • 4. Get enough protein to maintain your muscle. Aim for 20=30 grams with each meal

  • 5. Consider supplements like green tea extract, conjugated linoleic acid or fat burners to boost your metabolism. But check with your doctor first, especially if you’re on any medications.



In the end, whether you bulk or cut first depends on your goals and current physique. The most important thing is that you have a plan and stick to it. With hard work and consistency, you’ll get the body you want.



How To Calculate Your Calorie Needs for Cutting and Bulking



To successfully cut or bulk, you need to determine how many calories you need each day to achieve your goals. The most accurate way is to calculate your total daily energy expenditure or TDEE. This represents how many calories you burn per day based on your activity level.



Calculate your TDEE



  • 1. Determine your basal metabolic rate or BMR. This is the number of calories you burn at rest per day. You can use a online BMR calculator to determine your specific number based on factors like your age, gender, height, and weight.
  • 2. Factor in your activity level. Take your BMR and multiply it by an activity factor:
    • • Sedentary (little or no exercise): BMR x 1.2

    • • Lightly active (light exercise 1-3 days/week): BMR x 1.375

    • • Moderately active (moderate exercise 3-5 days/week): BMR x 1.55

    • • Very active (vigorous exercise 6-7 days/week): BMR x 1.725

    • • Extremely active (intense exercise multiple times per day, physical job): BMR x 1.9

      • 1. The result is your total daily energy expenditure or TDEE. This is the average number of calories you need each day to maintain your current weight based on your activity level.


Adjusting for cutting or bulking



  • • to cut, aim for a calorie deficit of about 500 calories per day from your TDEE. This will result in about 1 pound of weight loss per week. Adjust as needed to continue losing at your goal rate.

  • • To bulk, aim for a calorie surplus of about250-500 calories per day from your TDEE. This will result in about ½ to 1 pound of weight gain per week. Make sure to also increase your protein intake to build muscle. Adjust as needed to gain at your goal rate.

  • • Recalculate your TDEE every 4-6 weeks as your activity levels or stats change. Precision is key for the best results. Minor tweaks can make a big difference.

The keys to success are determining an accurate starting point, making small adjustments over time based on your progress, and tracking your results to stay on the path to your ultimate physique goals. Consistency and patience are virtues when cutting or bulking. You’ve got this!

Best Exercises for Cutting and Bulking



When it comes to exercise, cutting and bulking refer to phases aimed at either losing fat or gaining muscle. The exercise you focus on during these phases will differ to optimize your results.



Cutting Exercises



For cutting, the goal is to trim excess fat from your body while maintaining muscle. Focus on high-intensity interval training (HIIT) and resistance training. Some of the best exercises for cutting include:



  • • Burpees: this full-body exercise raises your heart rate and burns major calories. Aim for 3 sets of 10-20 burpees, 2-3 times a week.

  • • Kettlebell swings: this powerful exercise works your entire posterior chain. Do 3 sets of 10-20 swings, 2-3 times a week. Start with a lighter weight and focus on proper form.

  • • Battle ropes: slamming battle ropes is a killer cardio exercise that ignites your core and upper body. Do 3-4 intervals of 30-60 seconds slamming, with 30-60 seconds of rest in between.

  • • Barbell complexes: pick 3-4 compound barbell lifts like bent-over rows, overhead presses, squats and lunges. Do a set of each exercise back-to-back with little or no rest. Repeat for 3-5 rounds. This intense routine will skyrocket your metabolism.



Bulking exercises



For bulking, the goal is to stimulate muscle growth by progressive overload. Focus on heavy compound lifts with longer rest periods. Some of the best exercises for bulking include:



  • • Deadlifts: this exercise works your entire backside and core. Aim for 3-4 sets of 5-8 reps of deadlifts, 2 times a week. Use a weight that fatigues your muscle in the target rep range.

  • • Bench press; the bench press is the king of chest exercises. Do 3-4 sets of 5-8 reps, 2 times a week. Make sure to push the weight up in a slow, controlled motion and squeeze your chest at the top

  • • Squats: squats are essential for building powerful legs and toned butt. Do 3-4 sets of 5-8 reps, 2 times a week. Descend into a squat until your thighs are parallel to the floor and drive back up to the starting position.

  • • Pull-ups: pull-ups are one of the best exercises for building a wide, thick back. Aim for 3 sets to failure, 2 times a week. Use a band for assistance if needed and build up your strength over time.


So, whether you want to cut fat or gain mass, focus on the right combination of exercises, intensity, volume, and nutrition. Consistency is key!




Supplements That Can Help with Cutting and Bulking



When cutting or bulking, supplements can give you an extra boost to maximize your results. Here are some of the top supplements to consider:



Protein powder



Whether cutting or bulking, protein powder is essential. When cutting, protein powder helps you maintain muscle mass. When bulking, it provides the building blocks to gain muscle. Look for a powder with at least 20-3- grams of protein per scoop and minimal sugar and fat. Whey and casein are popular options.



BCAAs


Branched-chain amino acids or BCAAs, which include leucine, isoleucine, and valine, make up about 1/3 of muscle protein. Supplementing with BCAAs helps prevent muscle breakdown when cutting and stimulates muscle protein synthesis when bulking. Take 5-10 grams of BCAAs with your pre- and post- workout shake.



Creatine



Creatine is one of the most studied sports supplements. it works by increasing the body’s store of phosphocreatine, an energy source for high-intensity exercise. Creatine can help boost strength and muscle gain when bulking. When cutting, it may help maintain strength and performance. Aim for 3-5 grams of creatine monohydrate per day.



Fat Burners



Fat burners containing ingredients like green tea extract, cayenne pepper, and conjugated linoleic acid (CLA) may give your metabolism an extra boost when cutting. However, the effects in some people like insomnia, anxiety, and increased heart rate. So, start with a low dose and see how you respond.



Tips to Maximize Muscle Growth While Bulking



To maximize your muscle gains while bulking, follow these tips:



Stay hydrated



Drink plenty of water to aid muscle recovery and gain. Aim for 6-8 glasses per day as a general rule of thumb. Your urine should be pale yellow – if its dark, you need to hydrate more.



Get Enough sleep



Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep per night. Sleep is when your muscles recover and grow. Lack of sleep can hamper gains and lead to overtraining.



Limit Cardio



While bulking, limit excessive cardio which can burn calories and hinder gains. Aim for 2-3 short sessions per week at a moderate intensity. Walking, biking and recreational sports are good options.



Following these tips will put you on the fast track to packing on quality muscle mass. Be consistent, train hard, and fuel your body – the gains will come!



Common Cutting and Bulking Mistakes to Avoid



Cutting and bulking successfully requires avoiding some common mistakes. Its easy to get caught up in extremes, but for the best results, moderation and consistency are key.



Not tracking calories accurately



To cut or bulk effectively, you need to know your daily calorie needs and adjust based on your goals. Not properly tracking what you eat and drink each day is a recipe for wasted effort. Use a food scale and an app like MyFitnessPal to log everything that passes your lips. Be honest with yourself-it’s the only way to make progress.



Not adjusting calories gradually



Don’t slash your calories by 1000 per day to cut or double them to bulk. Make adjustments of no more than 500 calories at a time. This allows your body and mind to adapt slowly to changes in food intake. More gradual approaches lead to sustainable success.



Not adjusting macros



In addition to calories, pay attention to the balance of protein, carbs and fat you’re eating, known as your macros. When cutting, keep protein high to maintain muscle, lower carbs and fat. When bulking, increase protein along with carbs and healthy fats. Adjust your macros every few weeks based on how your body is responding.



Not doing resistance training



Both cutting and bulking require resistance or strength training to shape your physique. Without it, any weight lost or gained won’t provide the desired results. Lift weights 2-4 times a week, focusing on compound exercises like squats, deadlifts, bench press and overhead press.



Not staying hydrated



whether cutting or bulking, drink plenty of water every day to keep your body functioning properly. Dehydration negatively impacts your workouts, recovery, and metabolism. As a general rule, aim for about half your body weight in ounces as a minimum. Also, drink more water on workout days to account for fluid loss through sweat.



By avoiding these common mistakes, staying consistent and making gradual changes over time, you’ll be cutting and bulking like a pro in no time. But remember-patience and moderation are key. Don’t get caught up in extremes. Keep your goals realistic and sustainable for the best long-term success.


Conclusion



So, there you have it, the ultimate guide to navigating the tricky world of cutting and bulking. The key is finding the right balance for your goals and sticking with a plan. Don’t get discouraged if you don’t see immediate results. Building the body, you want takes dedication over the long haul. But stay focused on your goal, push through the difficult days, and keep putting one foot in front of the other. You’ll get there. Whether you’re cutting to reveal those hard-earned gains or bulking to pack on more muscle, keep your head in the game and your eyes on the prize. You’ve got this! Now go hit the gym and make it happen. The results will be worth it.
